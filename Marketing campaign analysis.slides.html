Telemarketing campaign analysis¶

by Liudmila Danilovskaya¶

The purpose of investigation is to:¶

  • understand the results of the previous campaigns

  • find the trends and relations between features and target variable

  • find the factors which facilitate clients to start saving agreements

The dataset overview¶

Data were gathered from 2 datasets:

  • customer_info.csv
  • call_info.csv

Merging these datasets resulted in a dataset of 20 features.

The following features were analysed:

  • sale - target variable ('yes', 'no')
  • last contact duration
  • outcome of the previous campaign
  • days since last contacted in previous campaign
  • number of contacts in the previous campaign
  • consumer confidence index
  • day of week of last contact
  • month of last contact
  • client's age
  • client's education
  • client's occupation

Convertion rate by last contact duration¶

The longer contact with the client the higher chances of their conversion.

Conversion rate by outcome of the previous campaigns¶

Outcome of the previous compaign impacts the conversion rate of the current campaign. If previous campaign was successful, a new campaign has more converted customers than not converted.

Days since last contacted in previous campaign¶

Chances of conversion is higher if time from the last contact is less.

Conversion rate by the number of contacts during the previous campaign¶

Conversion rate and consumer confidence index by month¶

Consumer confidence index contributes to the conversion rate.

Conversion rate by day of week¶

Day of week doesn't show considerable effect on the conversion rate.

Age distribution for those who started savings agreement¶

Most of the customers who started saving agreements were in the age of 25-45, while the mean age is 40 years old.

Conversion rate by education¶

Most of the clients have higher education. Higher education contributes to the higher conversion rate.

Conversion rate by occupation¶

Clients working on administrative or technical positions have higher conversion rate rather than students, unemployed or housemaid.

Conclusion¶

Factors contributing to the higher conversion rate:

  • longer contact with the client

  • success outcome of the previous cammpaign

  • minimum number of days since las contact

  • number of contact since lst campaign

  • high consumer confidence index

  • young age

  • higher education

  • well-paid and stable job
